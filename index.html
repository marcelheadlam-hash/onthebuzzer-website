WHY A GAMESHOW

Every failed corporate event has the same root cause. The format asked too much of the people in the room. Gameshows work because competition is a universal language. It does not require anyone to open up, share, or perform on demand. The structure creates the energy. The rounds create the moments. People engage because they want to — not because they have been told to.

THE FORMAT IS FAIR. THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING.

Seniority is irrelevant. Whether you are the CEO or a new starter, speed on the buzzer and strategic wit are the only currencies that matter. The format levels the room without anyone having to be told to participate.

Six rounds. Each one designed to surface something different in your team.

THE SPRINT

Rapid-fire questions. Speed determines everything. Sets the competitive tone from the first buzzer. First team to buzz in answers. No conferring, no second chances.

DELEGATION

Teams nominate their answer. Reveals who trusts whom and who has been underestimated. One person speaks for the whole team. Choose wisely.

THE COLLECTIVE

No individual contributions count. The team must synchronise to solve. The room shifts. Every member must agree before an answer is submitted. Consensus under pressure.

THE WAGER

Teams stake accumulated points on a single answer. Risk calculation exposes the culture. Go big and you can close the gap. Go big and get it wrong and the game changes completely.

THE CONSENSUS

Predict what the majority of the room will say. Strategy over knowledge. There is no right answer — only the most popular one. Reading the room is the skill.

THE BOARD

The finale. Teams go head to head predicting the most popular answers to a survey question. The higher your answer ranked, the more points you take. Get it wrong and hand the advantage to your opponents.
