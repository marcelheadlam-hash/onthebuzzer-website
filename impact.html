Giving Back

Competition as a
Force for Good.

On The Buzzer uses the energy of competition to raise money for causes that matter. Every event is an opportunity to give back.

The Philosophy

Games Are the Most Powerful Tool for Raising Money.

Giving back is built into the format. Competition brings people together, lowers their guard, and creates a room full of people who are genuinely present — and that is precisely the environment in which generosity flourishes.

We believe games are one of the most underused vehicles for doing good. The structure holds attention, the competition creates stakes, and the collective experience gives people something to give toward together.

We have used the format to raise money for causes we believe in. Movember was one of them. The evening raised thousands of pounds and the room understood immediately why the cause and the format were a natural fit.

This is not a one-off. Giving back is a core part of how On The Buzzer operates, and we intend to keep doing it.

Why It Works

Why Competition Works
Where Other Formats Don't.

Full Attention

A competitive format holds the room in a way that a speaker or a raffle never does. Every person is engaged from the first question to the last. That attention is the most valuable thing a fundraiser can have.

Shared Experience

When people experience something together — the tension of a close round, the drama of a final question — they feel a collective investment in the moment. Giving becomes part of that shared experience rather than a separate ask.

Real Energy

Competition creates genuine reactions. Laughter, surprise, celebration. That energy does not need to be manufactured or prompted. It exists naturally — and it makes people more receptive, more generous, and more connected to a cause.
