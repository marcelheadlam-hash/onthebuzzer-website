The Format

Why A Gameshow

Gameshows work because competition is a universal language. The structure creates the energy. The rounds create the moments. People connect with each other naturally — not because they have been told to, but because the format gives them a reason to.

Real competition produces real reactions. And real reactions are the foundation of genuine connection between people who share a workplace.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many people can you accommodate?

We host corporate gameshows for groups of 20 to 300+ people. Most clients book for 50–150 attendees. The format scales — whether you have 30 or 250, the competitive energy works.

What's included in the price?

Professional host and co-host, all equipment (buzzers, screens, sound system), setup, technical support, and post-event breakdown. No hidden fees. For Staff Engagement, gaming stations and a resident DJ are also included.

How long does a typical show run?

90–120 minutes for our standard format. We also offer 30–60 minute segments for conferences or agendas. For Staff Engagement, the full evening (show + gaming + DJ) typically runs 3–4 hours.

Where do you operate?

Across the UK. We bring all equipment to your venue — office, hotel, conference centre, or external event space. London, Manchester, Birmingham, Leeds, Edinburgh — wherever your event is.

How far in advance should we book?

Most clients book 4–6 weeks ahead. For peak dates (December, July) or groups over 100 people, we recommend 8+ weeks. If your date is soon, contact us and we'll confirm availability within 24 hours.

What makes this different from a quiz night?

Broadcast-quality production, professional hosting, inclusive game design (no pub quiz trivia), and full logistics management. Built for corporate environments. Everyone plays, everyone contributes — no trivia experts needed.

Can you customise the content for our industry or company?

Yes. We take a brief before every event. If there are topics relevant to your team, department, or culture that you want woven into the content, tell us and we'll build them in.

What does the venue need to provide?

A screen or projector, a power source, and enough space for teams of 4–6 people seated at tables. We bring everything else — buzzers, laptop, scoring system, speakers, and all content.
